Seaweed

Seaweed is a type of marine algae that grows along rocky coastlines and in shallow ocean waters around the world.

It comes in various colors such as green, brown, and red, and can range in size from tiny filaments to large kelp forests.

Seaweed plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by providing food, oxygen, and shelter for many ocean animals.

It also absorbs carbon dioxide, helping to keep the ocean healthy.
