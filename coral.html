Coral

Coral are tiny marine animals that live together in large colonies on the ocean floor.

Each coral, called a polyp, produces a hard outer skeleton made of calcium carbonate.

Over many years, these skeletons accumulate and form massive coral reefs.

Coral reefs are often called the “rainforests of the sea” because they provide shelter, food, and breeding grounds for thousands of marine species.

They also protect coastlines from strong waves and erosion, making them one of the most important ecosystems on Earth.

Ways to Protect

1. Avoid touching or stepping on corals:

Physical contact can damage their fragile structures.

2. Support coral conservation efforts:

Join or donate to organisations working to restore coral reefs.

3. Avoid buying coral products:

Don’t purchase jewellery or decorations made from coral.
