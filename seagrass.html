Seagrass

Seagrass is a type of flowering plant that lives fully submerged in shallow coastal waters.

Unlike seaweed, which is an algae, seagrass has roots, stems, and leaves, just like land plants.

It forms large underwater meadows that serve as vital habitats for many marine animals, such as fish, sea turtles, and dugongs.

Seagrass also helps keep the ocean healthy by stabilising the sea floor, reducing coastal erosion, and producing oxygen through photosynthesis.

Additionally, it absorbs carbon dioxide, making it an important part of the fight against climate change.

Ways to Protect

1. Reduce pollution:

Avoid dumping waste, oil, or chemicals into the sea. Use fewer fertilisers and pesticides, as these can wash into the ocean and harm seagrass.

2. Prevent coastal damage:

Support responsible coastal development and avoid activities that destroy seagrass beds, such as dredging or land reclamation.

3. Anchor carefully:

Boaters should use designated anchoring areas or mooring buoys to prevent anchors from tearing up seagrass.
