Clownfish

Clownfish are small, brightly colored fish commonly found in warm, shallow waters of coral reefs, especially in the Indian and Pacific Oceans.

They are best known for their striking orange colour with white stripes, though some species can be red, yellow, or black.

Clownfish live in a symbiotic relationship with sea anemonesâ€”the anemone protects with its stinging tentacles, while the clownfish keeps the anemone clean and provides nutrients to it.

Clownfish are also popular in aquariums and are well-known from movies and media.

They are social fish, often living in small groups with a strict hierarchy.

Ways to Protect

1. Protect coral reefs:

Avoid activities that damage reefs, like coral mining or careless snorkelling.

2. Avoid overfishing:

Do not capture clownfish from the wild for aquariums unsustainably.

3. Reduce carbon emissions:

Slowing climate change helps prevent coral bleaching and habitat loss.
