Sea Turtle

Sea turtles are large marine reptiles that have existed for over 100 million years.

They live in oceans around the world, mainly in tropical and subtropical waters.

Sea turtles have a hard, protective shell, flippers for swimming, and a diet that varies by speciesâ€”some eat jellyfish and seaweed, while others eat seagrass or crustaceans.

They play an important role in maintaining healthy marine ecosystems, such as keeping seagrass beds and coral reefs balanced.

Sea turtles face threats from habitat loss, pollution, fishing nets, and climate change, making conservation efforts critical for their survival.

Ways to Protect

1. Protect nesting beaches:

Avoid disturbing nests and reduce beachfront lighting at night.

2. Reduce plastic pollution:

Keep oceans free from plastic bags and debris that turtles can ingest.

3. Use turtle-safe fishing practices:

Employ nets and hooks designed to prevent accidental capture.
