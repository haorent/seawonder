Kelp

Kelp is a large, brown type of seaweed that grows in dense underwater forests near coastlines, especially in cold, nutrient-rich waters.

It is one of the fastest-growing plants in the world, capable of growing up to half a meter per day.

Kelp forests create vital habitats for many marine animals, including fish, sea otters, seals, and crustaceans, providing them with food and shelter.

Besides supporting marine life, kelp also plays a key role in maintaining ocean health by producing oxygen and absorbing carbon dioxide.

Humans harvest kelp for various uses, such as food (like kelp noodles), fertilisers, medicines, and even everyday products like toothpaste, ice cream, and shampoo.

Ways to Protect

1. Reduce carbon emissions:

Slowing climate change helps keep ocean temperatures stable for kelp growth.

2. Control invasive species:

Prevent non-native species from damaging kelp habitats.

3. Promote marine protected areas:

Support ocean zones where kelp forests and marine life are safe.
